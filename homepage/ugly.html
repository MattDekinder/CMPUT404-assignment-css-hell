Who am I?

I am a fourth-year computing science student and the University of Alberta. My interests lie in creating useful products which have a meaningful impact. I enjoy finding solutions to unique technical problems and all forms of work which require creativity. Outside of programming, I enjoy brewing beer and wine in eternal pursuit of artisanal quality.

History

I worked on a year-long internship for LegacyX Software as technical support on a small team of 9 people. This role exposed me to direct client interaction and management as well as how ot participate in a scrum team. I supported and regularly built/deployed a C# program on client servers. This was a small enough company that I got exposure to a wide variety of roles and tasks, ranging from mundane ad-hoc sql database modification per support requests to bug fixes and feature addition.

What I offer

I bring a fair amount of experience in how to view the differences between a non-technical client's requests and how to best implement their vision such that requirements are met, while remaining open to probable subsequent changes. I am very interested in the "big picture" of how my software will affect a client's business, subsequent support for that product and subsequent feature additions. I hate doing work twice for any reason! This is a great source of strength for how I approach projects-- rather than head-down technical development, I hope to understand the larger picture and make the right decisions the first time.
